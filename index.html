Portfolio

RUEquivalent

I developed a website designed to streamline the process for Rutgers students seeking course equivalencies across New Jersey's 18 community colleges. This platform significantly reduces the time students would otherwise spend navigating the NJ Transfer website, where they must manually search and scroll through numerous combinations of community colleges to identify the correct course equivalencies. To implement the website, I found the Rutgers courses API in JSON format. Then, I used Selenium and Beautiful Soup to scrape the course equivalencies from the NJ Transfer website. I utilized OpenAI's GPT-3.5 model to generate course descriptions and the Google Maps API to get the distance from each community college to the user's location. One of the challenges I faced was scraping course equivalencies from the NJ Transfer website. I was attempting to submit a POST request to the website to get the course equivalencies but kept receiving the same response. Eventually, I used Selenium to click the button, which resolved the issue. This project has taught me to be resourceful and made me willing to try different approaches to solve a problem, even if it doesn't initially appear that the new approach will resolve the issue.

Fire Extinguishing Bot

In this project, I tested various bots navigating a maze to reach a button while dealing with a spreading fire, using different algorithms to handle the challenge. Bot 1 employs Breadth-First Search to plan a static path to the button, avoiding only the initial fire cell and ignoring the spread of fire. Bot 2 also uses BFS but adapts dynamically by re-planning the shortest path at each step, avoiding current fire cells. Bot 3 further refines this approach by considering both current and adjacent fire cells when re-planning, and if no viable path is found, it resorts to planning based on the current fire cells. Bot 4 utilizes the A* algorithm with a Pythagorean distance heuristic to re-plan the path at each step, effectively navigating around current fire cells. After developing each bot, I tested the performance of each bot under different flamabilities of the fire. This project not only demonstrates my understanding of various algorithms and adaptive strategies but also showcases my proficiency in Java and effective software methodology, applying these principles to solve complex, real-world problems in a structured and systematic manner.

Health Journey

I collaborated on the backend development of a web application designed to enhance access to healthcare for elderly patients and individuals with urgent medical needs. Leveraging NJ Transit data, this innovative tool enables users to quickly locate the nearest medical facilities at each bus stop along their chosen route. Our backend implementation involved extracting and processing data for 253 bus routes and over 16,000 bus stops from NJ Transit. This comprehensive dataset allowed us to create a user-friendly interface where individuals can easily visualize the location of medical facilities throughout their journey, simplifying the process of finding care while using public transportation. Midway through the hackathon, my partner and I encountered issues extracting the NJ Transit data from the provided dataset and manipulating it to get the correct data we needed. I had never worked with Node.js or Express.js before, so I faced a steep learning curve. Nevertheless, I was able to resolve the issues with my teammates by using online documentation and discussing the problems with my partner. We also sought guidance from a project manager at NJ Transit whom we met at the event. Through constant communication and iterative testing, we successfully launched a functional prototype. This experience taught me the importance of teamwork, leveraging individual strengths, and seeking external help when needed. It reinforced that collaboration and adaptability are key to overcoming complex challenges.This project was recognized for its potential impact and innovative use of NJ Transit data. We were honored to receive the "Best Use of NJ Transit Data" award at HackRU Fall 2023 and had the privilege of presenting our idea to NJ Transit leadership.
